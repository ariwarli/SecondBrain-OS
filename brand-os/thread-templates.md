## Hook Library

### Hook Criteria #1: STUPID & SHORT

**Prinsip:**
- Otak butuh kalori untuk berpikir
- Jika perceived value < calories usage → copy diabaikan
- Harus bisa dipahami anak 12 tahun

**Logic (pseudo-code):**
```
if (perceived_value > calories_usage) read_copy()
else ignore_copy()
```

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Replace complex words dengan simple words
- [ ] Remove anything yang tidak essential
- [ ] Anak 12 tahun harus bisa paham
- [ ] Tanya: "Why would it matter to them?"
- [ ] Hindari "our/I" — gunakan "you/your"

**Examples:**
| ❌ Before | ✅ After |
|-----------|----------|
| Our revolutionary product can write blog posts super-fast | Write blog posts fast |
| The best NextJS boilerplate | Launch your startup in days, not months |
| The AI-powered voice note app | Turn your thoughts into actionable notes |

---

### Hook Criteria #2: BE PRECISE

**Prinsip:**
- Hindari "fancy words"
- Buat customer bisa VISUALIZE produk/benefit
- Gunakan angka dan unit spesifik

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Avoid buzzwords
- [ ] Use numbers & units instead of generic terms ("fast" → "in days")
- [ ] Use words yang bikin orang visualize rasanya pakai produk

**Examples:**
| ❌ Before | ✅ After |
|-----------|----------|
| Transform Your Content Game, effortless and powerful | Create captivating Reddit-like videos in seconds |
| Ship your startup fast | Ship your startup in days, not weeks |
| This car is super silent | At 60 mph, the loudest noise in the new Rolls-Royce comes from the electric clock |

---

### Hook Criteria #3: AVOID NEGATIONS

**Prinsip:**
- Negasi = mental gymnastics = burn calories
- Kalimat positif lebih mudah dibaca
- Boleh pakai negasi, tapi positive sentence biasanya lebih baik

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Jika menulis negasi, coba ubah jadi kalimat positif

**Example:**
| ❌ Before | ✅ After |
|-----------|----------|
| The hosting solution that won't cost you lots of money | The cheapest hosting solution |

## Format & Struktur

### Vertical Space Rules
- Gunakan line break antara setiap bagian
- Setiap tweet harus punya breathing room
- Jangan stack paragraf tanpa jeda

### Tweet Numbering
- Gunakan format: `1/`, `2/`, dst.
- Hook TIDAK diberi nomor
- CTA di akhir bisa diberi nomor atau tidak

### Engagement Elements
- Emoji: 🧵 untuk thread indicator
- Bullet points: • untuk list outcomes
- Hook harus PROVOCATIVE

### Output Rules
- Output HANYA thread, tanpa penjelasan tambahan
- Preserve SEMUA key insight dari source
- Concise, easy to digest, direct, simple words

## Contoh Thread Terbaik

### EXAMPLE 1: Tesla Open-Source Patents

**HOOK:**
```
Elon Musk open-sourced all of Tesla's patents for other car companies to copy.

Wall Street called it 'dumb' and thought Tesla will be crushed.

Elon was right. Everyone was wrong.

🧵 Every entrepreneur needs to understand why he did it and why it worked:
```

**THREAD:**
```
1/ Look at this graph 👇
In 2014, EVs made up less than 1% of car sales in the US.
But Elon realized that his competition wasn't other car companies.
It was the internal combustion engine itself.

2/ To win, Tesla needed to change the entire automotive landscape.
Elon's strategy was simple: Make it easier for everyone to build electric cars.
By doing this, Tesla removed barriers for other companies to enter the EV market.
This wasn't just altruism. It was a brilliant plan.

3/ Elon understood the Positive-Sum game
In a Positive-Sum game, all participants can benefit, creating value that didn't previously exist.
By open-sourcing its patents, Tesla encouraged more companies to develop EVs.

4/ This didn't just benefit those companies.
It created a larger, more vibrant EV market.
More EVs meant more charging stations, better infrastructure, and greater public interest.
Ultimately, Tesla benefits from a growing EV ecosystem.

5/ It positioned Tesla as an industry leader
By freely sharing patents, Tesla took the position of industry benefactor.
This bold move garnered massive media attention and public goodwill.
Tesla wasn't just selling cars. It was selling a better vision of the future.

6/ Finally, it created a competitive scenario.
As more companies entered the EV market, they inadvertently promoted the concept of electric cars.
This expanded the overall market, with Tesla leading the charge.
Sometimes, a rising tide really does lift all boats (or cars ;)

7/ The gamble paid off for Musk:
• More car companies entered the EV market
• Investment in charging infrastructure increased
• Public interest in EVs grew
• Tesla maintained its position as the EV leader

8/ Elon Musk's open-source gambit proves an important point:
Sometimes, the best way to win is to change the game entirely.
By focusing on growing the pie instead of fighting for slices, Tesla reshaped an entire industry.
And that's how you lead a revolution.
```

**CTA:**
```
9/ RT the first tweet if you found this thread valuable.
Follow me @[handle] for more threads on entrepreneurship, investing and human performance.
```

### EXAMPLE 2: Sam Altman UBI Study

**HOOK:**
```
BREAKING: The results of Sam Altman's massive $60M "Universal Basic Income" study are in.

He gave 1,000 people $1,000 every month for 3 years to see if UBI could help as AI steals more jobs.

The results? Absolutely fascinating.

Here's the story 🧵:
```

**THREAD:**
```
1/ Imagine a world where robots and AI do most of the jobs.
What happens to all the people who lose their jobs to machines?
That's where Universal Basic Income (UBI) comes in - it's like an allowance for adults, given by the government to everyone, regardless of their work status.

2/ Sam Altman (OpenAI CEO) just completed a massive experiment to see if UBI works.
He raised $60 million to give 1,000 people (w/ annual incomes under $28k) $1,000 every month for 3 years.
His goal?
To explore how UBI might help when AI takes over more jobs in the future.

3/ The results are in, and they're absolutely fascinating.
People used the extra cash mostly for essentials:
• Food
• Rent
• Transportation
Spending increased by $310/month.
They saved a bit more, too.
But here's the interesting part: they actually worked less over time.

4/ This doesn't mean they became lazy.
Instead, people got more selective about their jobs, looking for work they actually enjoyed.
It's as if the basic income gave them a chance to ask, "What do I really want to do?" rather than just taking any job to make ends meet.

5/ However, there's an important catch.
The positive effects - less stress, more food security - faded after the first year.
This suggests that while UBI can provide immediate relief, it's not a complete solution to the challenges we might face in an AI-dominated future.

6/ So what does this mean as we look ahead?
It shows that just giving people money isn't enough.
If AI and robots take over many jobs, we'll need more than cash:
We'll need new ways for people to feel purposeful and fulfilled.

7/ Altman is already thinking about the next step.
Instead of just giving people money, he's considering the idea of "Universal Basic Compute" - giving everyone access to AI power.
Imagine having your own *free* AI assistant to help you create, learn, and solve problems.
```
